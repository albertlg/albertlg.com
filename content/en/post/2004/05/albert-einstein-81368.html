According to the R.A.E. ...

Relativity

1. n. The quality of being relative.

2. n. Phys. A theory that seeks to determine how physical laws change when the frame of reference is altered.

~ special

1. n. Phys. The theory formulated by the German scientist Einstein, based on the fact that light propagates independently of the motion of the body emitting it, and that there is no phenomenon capable of determining whether a body is at rest or in uniform rectilinear motion.


Relative

(From Latin relatīvus)

1. adj. Related to someone or something.

2. adj. Not absolute.

3. adj. Limited; not very much. He gave that matter a relative importance.

4. adj. Debatable; open to question. His opinion is very relative.

5. n. Gram. Relative pronoun.
