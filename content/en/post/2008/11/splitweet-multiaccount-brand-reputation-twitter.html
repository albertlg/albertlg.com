Last Monday, three colleagues and friends (@obokaman, @alombarte, and @albertlg) launched Splitweet.com, a project that came from our personal need to manage multiple accounts on Twitter and monitor mentions of certain keywords, names, or brands within this free microblogging service.

It took us less than a week to develop the first public beta version of Splitweet, and today, not even a week since it's been online, it's already translated (in addition to Spanish) into:

  • English: http://en.splitweet.com
  • Catalan: http://ca.splitweet.com
  • French: http://fr.splitweet.com

And we're expecting it to launch in German, Italian, and Portuguese very soon... really soon. We've also received a request to translate it into Dutch. Any other requests? Don't be shy. ;)

The tool is very straightforward, and it seems to be well-received, so I'd like to encourage you to try it out. Do you manage more than one Twitter account? Do you need to keep track of the contacts from each and every one of them at once? Read replies from all your accounts? Monitor brand mentions or specific keywords on Twitter? Look no further— Splitweet is your tool.

Ah! And if you “only” have one Twitter account, we're sure you'll also like Splitweet... it's purple! Haha... But seriously, we believe you can make good use of it too (its simplicity, its interface, its keyword monitoring...).

We'll continue improving Splitweet based on your needs, so don't think twice: if you have something to say, write us at staff@splitweet.com.
