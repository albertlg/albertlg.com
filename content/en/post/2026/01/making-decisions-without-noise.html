It’s hard to make good decisions when everything feels loud.
News, notifications, opinions, pressure, numbers, deadlines, expectations. The challenge isn’t collecting more information — it’s not drowning in it.

Over time I’ve built a small system: a 3-question filter I use to separate impulses from real decisions. It’s simple, repeatable, and works for work, family and everyday life.

1) What is truly at stake?

Many decisions look big… until you look closer.

This question helps me:

  • reduce drama,
  • avoid overthinking,
  • and match my energy to the real impact.

If little is at stake, I don’t treat it like an emergency. If a lot is, I act accordingly.

2) What do I actually control?

Some noise will always exist: expectations, emails, someone else’s urgency. I can’t control that.

But I can control:

  • the time I give it,
  • how I interpret it,
  • and the next step.

This keeps me active rather than reactive.

3) What will I want to remember about this decision a year from now?

This is the one that grounds me. Pulling the decision from NOW into the FUTURE quiets the ego and sharpens intention.

  • What will I wish I had prioritised?
  • What will I wish I had avoided?
  • What example do I want my daughters to see?

These questions bring perspective.

A small system that breathes

It’s not meant to be perfect. It’s simply a kind of internal rhythm that helps when noise and urgency take over.

When I use it, decisions stop feeling like a race. They become a process. Less impulse, more perspective.

A 3-question filter that, without me planning it, has become one of the things holding my clarity this 2026.
