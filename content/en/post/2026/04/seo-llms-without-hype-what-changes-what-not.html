In recent months, there has been a lot of talk about SEO for LLMs.

Perhaps too much.

There is excitement. There is fear. There are quick promises.

And quite a bit of hype.

I prefer to separate what truly changes from what remains the same.

What Actually Changes

First, the environment.

Generative answers are no longer experimental. They are a stable layer on top of search engines.

This has an important implication: users can now obtain synthesis without clicking.

The goal is no longer just to rank.

It becomes about being understandable enough to be reused.

Some practical consequences:

  • Greater importance of clear structures.
  • Precise definitions.
  • Well-organized lists.
  • Intentional question-and-answer formatting.
  • Strong semantic coherence.

It’s not about writing more.

It’s about writing in a better organized way.

What Does Not Change

The technical foundation remains intact.

  • Clean architecture.
  • Coherent internal linking.
  • Topical authority built over time.
  • Real experience behind the content.

Without that, citation is unlikely.

Models do not invent authority.

They inherit it from prior signals.

The Strategic Shift

Before, we competed for clicks.

Now, we also compete to become a reference.

This is a subtle but important shift.

Content must now be able to be:

  • Extracted.
  • Summarized.
  • Reformulated.
  • Understood without additional context.

This demands precision.

And precision demands craft.

Common Mistakes I’m Seeing

  • Removing classical processes assuming they no longer matter.
  • Creating inflated content hoping the model will “detect” it.
  • Looking for magic formulas.

There are no magic formulas.

There is structure. There is clarity. There is consistency.

No Drama Needed

SEO is not dead.

It has become more demanding.

More technical at its base.

More strategic at the top layer.

And paradoxically, more human.

Because in the end, what works best is what is best understood.

Practical Perspective

If I had to summarize it in one sentence:

Don’t optimize for the model.

Optimize so that any intelligent system — human or artificial — can understand your work without friction.

The rest is noise.
